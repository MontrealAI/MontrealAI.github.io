Professional Services and Proof Mission Terms

Commercial and delivery boundaries for MasterClasses, Design Labs, Proof Missions, and enterprise programs.

1. Engagement instrument

Scope, fees, dates, deliverables, assumptions, dependencies, reviewers, data boundaries, acceptance criteria, and payment milestones must be set out in a signed statement of work or order form.

2. Nature of the work

Services may produce mission designs, Proof Debt registers, job queues, Evidence Dockets, reviewer packages, decision briefs, Action Graphs, Chronicle recommendations, or candidate Validated Skills. These are evidence and decision-support artifacts, not guaranteed positive findings or certifications.

3. Valid negative outcomes

A valid result may be repair, narrowing, rejection, quarantine, pause, or stop. Fees purchase the agreed work, professional time, and artifacts—not a favorable verdict.

4. Customer responsibilities

The customer must provide accurate inputs, authorized access, timely decisions, qualified personnel, applicable notices and permissions, and any required domain, legal, medical, security, tax, accounting, or regulatory reviewers.

5. Third-party reviewers

Independent reviewers should disclose conflicts and remain free to reject, narrow, or condition claims. Their compensation must not be contingent on a positive result unless clearly permitted and disclosed.

6. Intellectual property

Customer data remains customer data. Mission-specific deliverables and usage rights are governed by the signed instrument. GoalOS methodologies, templates, schemas, software, generalized know-how, and pre-existing materials remain with their applicable rightsholders unless expressly assigned in writing.

7. Fees and expenses

Deposits, milestones, taxes, travel, specialist costs, cloud or compute costs, and late-payment terms must be stated in the commercial instrument. No token, forecast, or presentation guarantees future value or return.

8. No professional advice or certification

Unless the signed instrument expressly names a properly qualified professional acting in that capacity, the services do not provide legal, tax, investment, medical, engineering, cybersecurity, regulatory, audit, attestation, or certification services.
