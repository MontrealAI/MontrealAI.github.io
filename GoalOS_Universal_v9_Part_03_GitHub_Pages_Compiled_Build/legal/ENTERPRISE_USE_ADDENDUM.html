Enterprise Use Addendum

Additional terms for organizations, teams, professional users, and private deployments.

Counsel review required. This addendum is a production template and must be reviewed against the actual operator, customer, jurisdictions, data, services, and commercial arrangement before execution.

1. Scope

This Addendum supplements the Terms of Use when the software, materials, or services are evaluated, deployed, or used by or for an organization. If a signed master agreement or order form conflicts with this Addendum, the signed instrument controls for the parties and subject matter it expressly covers.

2. Authority and administration

The organization is responsible for appointing authorized administrators, controlling devices and access, maintaining current user lists, and ensuring that each user has authority to process the information entered into the system.

3. Deployment classification

  • Public deployment: public or non-confidential information only.
  • Browser-local deployment: single-device processing; the organization remains responsible for endpoint security, backups, and browser storage.
  • Private hosted deployment: the organization must provide authentication, access logging, encryption, monitoring, incident response, and retention controls appropriate to the data.
  • Restricted deployment: use only in an approved controlled or air-gapped environment.

4. Data and confidentiality

Do not place regulated, privileged, secret, export-controlled, health, financial-account, authentication, private-key, seed-phrase, or other restricted data into a public GitHub Pages or ordinary public IPFS deployment. The organization determines its lawful basis, notices, retention, access, deletion, and cross-border requirements.

5. AI and proof boundary

GoalOS may help structure objectives, claims, evidence, risks, reviewers, and actions. It does not independently certify truth, legal compliance, safety, suitability, investment merit, medical appropriateness, cybersecurity, or regulatory approval. Human and qualified professional review remain required where applicable.

6. Acceptance and production authority

A generated plan, Evidence Docket, proof score, Chronicle record, or Validated Skill is not production authorization. The organization retains all authority and responsibility for deployment, contracts, payments, employment classification, taxes, security, and consequential actions.

7. Support and services

Professional services, external reviewers, custom integrations, hosted infrastructure, support levels, response times, and service credits exist only under a separately signed order, statement of work, or service-level schedule.

8. Audit and records

The organization should preserve applicable versions, acceptance receipts, source artifacts, reviewer reports, decisions, and change records. Browser-local records can be lost if storage is cleared or devices are replaced.
