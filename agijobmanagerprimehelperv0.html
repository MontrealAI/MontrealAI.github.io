What this computes

The commitment is the keccak256 hash of the exact packed values below:

keccak256(abi.encodePacked(procurementId, wallet, applicationURI, salt))
Best practice: once you commit on-chain, never change the wallet, URI, or salt. Reveal must reuse the exact same values.

1) Inputs

Enter your wallet, URI, and salt, then compute the commitment.

2) Outputs

Open write contract

3) One-screen checklist

Remember: the IPFS URI is used to build the commitment, but you do not paste the URI into the commitment field on Etherscan. You paste the 66-character 0x... commitment instead.

4) Super simple Etherscan steps

  1. Open the write-contract page.
  2. Connect the same wallet that you used in this helper.
  3. Find commitApplication.
  4. Paste the four values shown in Etherscan fields.
  5. Click Write and confirm in your wallet.
  6. Save your salt privately. You will need it later for reveal.

If the commit transaction fails because of allowance / transferFrom, approve the discovery contract as spender for the amount shown in the approval reference field, then retry the commit.

5) Reveal reminder

At reveal time, you must reuse the same wallet, same application URI, and same salt. If any one of those changes, reveal will fail.
