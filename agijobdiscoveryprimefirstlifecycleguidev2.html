What the job brief says, in one sentence

Premium discovery press-release procurement for AGIJobDiscoveryPrime, AGIJobManagerPrime, and ENSJobPages: tournament-style talent discovery, paid finalist trials, validator scoring, selected-slot settlement, and public IPFS memory.

Why this flow feels different

DiscoveryPrime is not just a post-and-pray job board. It stages the process: private signal first, public evidence later, a paid finalist round, then validator scoring before the job moves into AGIJobManagerPrime for execution and settlement.

Plain-English glossary

The core ideas without contract jargon.

Commit / reveal

First hide your application or score, then disclose it later. This makes early copying and crowd-following harder.

Finalist stipend

The paid amount for reaching the finalist trial round. In this procurement, that stipend is 1,500 AGIALPHA per valid finalist.

Validator bond

The amount a validator locks when submitting a score commitment. Here it is 350 AGIALPHA per score commitment.

Claimable balance

Money that sits inside the discovery contract waiting to be claimed. It is not automatically pushed to the user wallet.

Money, stakes, and rewards

What people care about most

This is the financial anatomy of the first premium discovery procurement.

Employer funds at creation

  • 100,000 AGIALPHA for the underlying job payout.
  • 7,020 AGIALPHA for discovery operations.
  • 4,500 AGIALPHA of that budget is reserved for finalist stipends.
  • 2,520 AGIALPHA of that budget is reserved for validator reveal rewards.

What applicants and finalists may lock

  • Application stake: 300 AGIALPHA.
  • Finalist total locked stake: 1,250 AGIALPHA.
  • For a shortlisted applicant who already locked the application stake, the usual top-up is 950 AGIALPHA.

What validators may lock and earn

  • Score bond per commitment: 350 AGIALPHA.
  • Reward budget per revealed score: 120 AGIALPHA.
  • The exact reward/refund depends on whether the finalist reached robust consensus and how close the revealed score lands to the median.

What generally comes back

  • Revealed but non-shortlisted applicants recover their application stake.
  • Valid finalists recover the locked finalist stake and receive the stipend.
  • Validators who reveal and score credibly can recover their bond and earn rewards.

What can be lost

  • An applicant who commits but does not reveal can lose the locked application stake.
  • A finalist who fails to accept or fails to submit a trial can lose the locked finalist stake.
  • A validator who commits but does not reveal can lose the score bond. Large deviations from consensus can also reduce bond refunds and rewards.

Two operational truths

  • Claiming is manual. Refunds, stipends, validator payouts, and unused employer balances become claimable inside the contract.
  • Advancing is public. Once a stage is ready, anyone can advance shortlist or winner logic. The lifecycle does not need to wait on a single operator.

What each participant should expect

Role guide

The easiest way to avoid a poor experience is for every role to know its own deadlines, stakes, and public visibility.

Employer

01

Creates the procurement, funds the payout and discovery budget, and sets the public tone.

Publish one clear, public-safe brief.
Announce every deadline in UTC and human language.
Recruit enough validators early enough that finalists can realistically reach 5 reveals.
Remember to claim unused budget or forfeited balances.

Applicant

02

Signals interest privately first, then reveals the actual application later.

Confirm eligibility and the premium reputation requirement before committing.
Save the exact application URI and salt used for the commitment.
Reveal before the deadline or the earlier commitment does not help you.
Assume public visibility for all revealed content.

Finalist

03

Gets a paid opportunity to show real work, but only by acting promptly.

Accept the shortlist on time and top up the locked stake if needed.
Submit the trial URI before the trial deadline.
Stay reachable after scoring in case a fallback promotion becomes possible.
Claim the returned stake and stipend after finalization.

Validator

04

Provides the quality signal that makes the finalist round mean something.

Commit scores independently, then reveal them honestly.
Missed reveals can cost the score bond entirely.
Median-based settlement rewards credible participation and punishes low-quality behavior.
Claim returned bond and earned rewards manually.

Smooth experience playbook

Best practices

These are the operational habits that make a premium discovery flow feel calm and premium instead of stressful and improvised.

1. Keep one canonical public brief
Use one stable job brief URI, and make sure it is appropriate for a public blockchain-linked process.
2. Announce deadlines like a concierge
Always communicate the UTC timestamp and the plain-English stage name, not just a raw Unix time.
3. Teach applicants to protect their salt
The reveal only works if the applicant kept the exact same salt and URI that were used to build the commitment.
4. Pre-line up validators
Do not wait until the trial phase ends to recruit validators. A finalist needs at least 5 revealed scores to be designatable.
5. Treat finalist acceptance as a separate moment
Being shortlisted is not enough. A finalist must still accept on-chain and maintain the full locked finalist stake.
6. Tell everyone that claims are manual
Returned stakes, stipends, validator payouts, and employer refunds are claimable balances, not automatic transfers.
7. Use a keeper or public operator
Because anyone can advance ready stages, a simple operator or community member can help keep the lifecycle moving.
8. Keep sensitive information off public URIs
The materials you supplied expressly warn that blockchain-linked content can be public. Do not embed confidential or personal data unless you accept that risk and have the rights to do so.

What happens if something goes wrong?

The contract is structured to keep moving, but it is also explicit about defaults and missed deadlines.

What if an applicant commits but never reveals?
That applicant will not compete for the shortlist. In this contract, an unrevealed application can forfeit its locked application stake to the employer during shortlist finalization, and the applicant also accumulates a discovery-default penalty that can hurt future historical scoring.
What if a shortlisted finalist does not accept, or accepts but never submits a trial?
The finalist falls out of contention. The locked finalist stake can become claimable by the employer, the finalist receives a discovery-default penalty, and the procurement continues with any remaining valid finalists.
What if validators commit scores but fail to reveal them?
Missing a reveal is costly. The unrevealed score bond can become claimable by the employer. This is one reason to recruit validators who actually plan to finish the whole scoring sequence.
What if there are too few validator reveals?
A finalist needs at least 5 revealed scores to become designatable. If no finalist reaches a designatable state, the procurement can close without a winner. DiscoveryPrime is willing to say “no winner” rather than pretend confidence without enough evidence.
What if the first designated finalist never takes the reserved slot?
That is what fallback promotion is for. If the selected-agent slot later expires while still unassigned, another eligible finalist with a valid composite score and enough reveals can be promoted into that slot.
Can the procurement still be cancelled?
Yes, but not arbitrarily forever. Under the code you supplied, the employer or protocol owner can cancel an unfinalized procurement so long as the conditions are satisfied, including that no finalist trial has yet been submitted and settlement pause constraints are not blocking the action.
Does the employer have to personally click every stage?
No. This is an important UX feature. Anyone can finalize the shortlist, finalize the winner when the window is over, and advance the procurement when the relevant conditions are met. That keeps the lifecycle from depending on one busy person.

After discovery: the AGIJobManagerPrime handoff

Selection is not the whole job

DiscoveryPrime discovers and designates. AGIJobManagerPrime then handles the reserved winner slot, the work period, the completion flow, and the final settlement logic.

What DiscoveryPrime is responsible for

  • Receiving the premium discovery configuration.
  • Running the commit / reveal application flow.
  • Shortlisting finalists and paying for the trial round.
  • Collecting validator scores and computing the composite ranking.
  • Designating the winning finalist into the selected-agent slot, with fallback promotion if needed.

What AGIJobManagerPrime takes over afterwards

  • The reserved selected-agent acceptance window: 3 days.
  • The configured checkpoint window: 2 days.
  • The underlying job duration: 7 days.
  • The later completion request, validator review, and settlement or dispute path under the job manager logic and the terms you supplied.
