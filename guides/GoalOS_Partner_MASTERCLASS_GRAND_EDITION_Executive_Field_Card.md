# GoalOS - Executive Field Card

## The one-line proposition

**GoalOS converts consequential objectives into proof-backed governed decision states - and admits only scoped, replayable capability to future work.**

## The institutional distinction

```text
A model may answer.
An agent may act.
An institution must prove.
```

## The proof-to-capability loop

```text
Objective
-> Mission Contract
-> Proof Debt
-> Custom AGI Jobs
-> ProofBundles
-> Evidence Docket
-> Validation
-> Chronicle
-> Validated Skill
-> Merkle Epoch
-> Mission 2
```

## What the partner receives

- Mission Contract and claims matrix
- Proof Debt register and custom proof jobs
- Evidence Docket and provenance
- verifier/reviewer record
- risk and cost ledgers
- executive brief and bounded decision
- Action Graph, challenge path, and rollback
- Chronicle recommendation
- scoped capability candidate
- public-safe proof room and private appendix index
- Mission 2 experimental design

## What makes GoalOS different

- Output never automatically earns authority.
- Missing proof becomes explicit work.
- Failed and negative evidence remain visible.
- Memory is a policy decision, not transcript storage.
- Private intelligence remains controlled; public proof uses minimal commitments.
- Merkle inclusion proves committed membership, not semantic truth.
- Open-ended exploration cannot bypass risk, evidence, baselines, replay, validation, Chronicle, or rollback.
- Compounding is tested on fresh work under equal constraints.

## Governed RSI

```text
TARGET -> EMIT -> FILTER -> ATLAS
-> TEST-PLAN -> EVAL -> INSERT -> PROMOTE
```

**Search control is not outcome authority.**

High novelty increases scrutiny. A Move-37 candidate must reproduce, survive stress tests, persist against baselines, and produce a decision-grade dossier.

## The founding partner ask

1. One consequential, bounded, reversible decision.
2. One accountable decision owner.
3. One reviewer with disclosed effective control.
4. One explicit data and privacy boundary.
5. One preregistered Mission 2.

## 30/60/90 evidence cadence

**30 days:** freeze mission, claims, proof level, reviewer route, data boundary; open the Docket.

**60 days:** complete proof jobs, replay, review, Chronicle decision, scoped capability, optional root packet.

**90 days:** run Mission 2 with Fresh Control, Raw Memory, Validated Skill, and ablation under equal constraints.

## Five board questions

1. What exactly earns authority?
2. What remains blocked?
3. Who independently reviewed it?
4. How can it be challenged or rolled back?
5. Does the admitted capability measurably improve the next mission?

## Claim boundary

This is a local/reference architecture and partner operating system. It does not establish achieved AGI/ASI, independent external validation, production authorization, live Mainnet status, legal/security/tax certification, token value, guaranteed outcome, or general recursive self-improvement.
