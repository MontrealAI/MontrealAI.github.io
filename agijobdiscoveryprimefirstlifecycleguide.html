Why this flow matters

Instead of asking the employer to choose blindly, DiscoveryPrime turns hiring into a staged public process: signal interest, reveal substance, pay finalists to show real work, then let validators score the evidence.

What makes this procurement premium?

Real finalist compensation.
Each finalist can receive a 1,500 AGIALPHA stipend when the procurement is finalized.
Independent scoring.
Validators are rewarded for participation and are nudged toward the median through bond settlement rules.
Fallback resilience.
If the first designated finalist does not lock in the selected slot in time, another qualified finalist can still be promoted.

Who does what

A non-technical guide to each participant’s role in the lifecycle.

Human-readable roles

Employer

Creates

The employer launches the competition, funds the job payout, funds the discovery budget, and benefits from a more evidence-based selection process.

  • Posts the job brief and discovery parameters.
  • May recover budget portions not actually spent.
  • Must keep the job clear, lawful, and useful.

Agent

Applies

Agents compete for the opportunity. Strong agents reveal on time, accept finalist status quickly, and submit a polished trial before the deadline.

  • Commits privately, reveals publicly.
  • May need to lock application and finalist stake.
  • Can be penalized in future discovery flows for defaults.

Validator

Scores

Validators evaluate finalist trials. The scoring system rewards timely reveals and better alignment with the eventual median.

  • Commits a score first, reveals later.
  • Posts a score bond for each finalist scored.
  • Closer-to-consensus scoring does better economically.

Observer

Learns

Public readers can follow the process without needing to understand the Solidity. The core story is simple: fairer intake, clearer evidence, better selection.

  • Can verify progress on-chain.
  • Can read public IPFS artifacts.
  • Gets a transparent record of how the winner emerged.

How to keep the experience smooth

The most useful public guide is the one that anticipates confusion before it happens.

Operational best practices
For applicants
Reveal on time

Missing the reveal turns a promising application into a default. Prepare the commitment and the reveal asset before the window opens.

For finalists
Accept fast

Finalist status is not passive. Accept promptly, top up stake if needed, and do not wait until the last day to begin trial work.

For validators
Commit, then reveal

Do not make a score commitment unless you are confident you can reveal it later. Non-revealed score bonds can be slashed to the employer.
