# Kernel v3 Use-Case Qualification Checklist

A mission is a strong Kernel v3 candidate when its consequence, evidence, and rollback burden justify constitutional controls.

1. Is there a named decision or action, not merely a request for content?
2. Could the output influence capital, production, policy, safety, reputation, settlement, or future model behavior?
3. Are primary sources, measurements, tests, or external evidence available?
4. Can success and failure be defined before execution?
5. Can the action surface be bounded by tools, data, budget, scope, and risk?
6. Can independent evaluation or challenge be introduced?
7. Is there a meaningful baseline or incumbent process?
8. Can irreversible action be deferred until human or institutional authorization?
9. Is a rollback, safe hold, dispute, or revision path possible?
10. Would a durable Evidence Docket reduce future review or reuse cost?
11. Can the public/private proof boundary be maintained?
12. Would failure be visible, publishable, and informative rather than silently hidden?

**Admission guidance:** a high-stakes mission with weak evidence availability should be narrowed, instrumented, or deferred - not made more autonomous.
