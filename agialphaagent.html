Second-order agency

Meta‑Agentic: Second‑Order Agency

Meta-agentic systems do not merely act. They create, select, evaluate, route, improve, and govern other agents and the rules under which those agents operate.

AGI Alpha Agent is the coordination layer where agents become an organized production system.

Definition: Meta‑Agentic describes an agent or system whose primary role is to create, select, evaluate, route, or reconfigure other agents and the rules governing their interactions — exercising second-order agency over a population of first-order agents.

The Three Clocks of Autonomous Coordination

Fast clock — Execution

Agents decompose work, call tools, write artifacts, run tests, retry failures, and package outputs.

Medium clock — Proof / Settlement

Proof bundles, validation, receipts, challenges, reputation, and settlement.

Slow clock — Governance / Promotion

Policy, disputes, upgrades, sovereign promotion, and archive law.

Proof‑First Ascension

No value without evidence. No autonomy without authority. No settlement without validation.

Agents can act, but value moves only when evidence survives validation. The archive preserves what worked so future mandates start stronger.

Machine speed belongs in execution. Trust belongs in proof. Permanence belongs in governance.
