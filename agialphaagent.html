Meta‑Agentic: Second-Order Agency

Meta-agentic systems do not merely act. They create, select, evaluate, route, improve, and govern other agents and the rules under which those agents operate.

Meta‑Agentic describes an agent or system whose primary role is to create, select, evaluate, route, or reconfigure other agents and the rules governing their interactions — exercising second-order agency over a population of first-order agents. env ∈ ENV_SET, optional; examples: alpha, x, …

gpu01.alpha.node.agi.eth

Sovereigns / AGI Businesses

Global sovereign: <sovereign>.agi.eth
Ecosystem root: env.agi.eth (examples: alpha.agi.eth, x.agi.eth)
AGI Business: <business>.env.agi.eth (example: ops.alpha.agi.eth)

Scope rule

entity.env.role.agi.eth is recognized and developed only within env.agi.eth.

Official environment package:

Optional aliases

The Three Clocks of Autonomous Coordination

Machine speed, trust discipline, and institutional permanence.

Fast clock — Execution

Agents decompose work, call tools, write artifacts, run tests, retry failures, and package outputs.

Medium clock — Proof / Settlement

Proof bundles, validation, receipts, challenges, reputation, and settlement.

Slow clock — Governance / Promotion

Policy, disputes, upgrades, sovereign promotion, and archive law.

Machine speed belongs in execution. Trust belongs in proof. Permanence belongs in governance.

AGI Alpha Agent scales autonomy by moving routine execution to the fast clock while keeping value movement and institutional promotion behind proof and governance.
