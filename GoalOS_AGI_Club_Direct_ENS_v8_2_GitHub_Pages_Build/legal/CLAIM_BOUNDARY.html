Claim Boundary

What this release demonstrates, what remains unproven, and how claims mature.

Permitted claim: GoalOS is an architecture and working browser-based operating surface for proof-gated commercial and autonomous-work design.

What is demonstrated

  • A static Web3 member-access flow based on MetaMask control and current direct ENS ownership.
  • A browser-local GoalOS Money Machine that can generate offers, pricing, funnels, proof-delivery structures, scenarios, and exports.
  • Research, specifications, implementation scaffolds, tests, and local or proxy evidence described in the applicable materials.

What is not established by this release

  • Achieved AGI, ASI, superintelligence, universal correctness, or autonomous sovereignty.
  • Guaranteed revenue, business success, valuation, token value, customer demand, safety, compliance, or production suitability.
  • Independent external validation, audited smart contracts, official benchmark superiority, legal certification, or reviewed Mainnet settlement unless separately evidenced.
  • Cryptographic secrecy of member files hosted as ordinary public static assets.

Evidence-promotion rule

Stronger claims require real tasks, baselines, ProofBundles, replay logs, cost and risk ledgers, external reviewer reports, delayed outcomes, and independent reproduction. Architecture, code, screenshots, activity counts, local tests, or a membership signature do not by themselves prove real-world impact.
