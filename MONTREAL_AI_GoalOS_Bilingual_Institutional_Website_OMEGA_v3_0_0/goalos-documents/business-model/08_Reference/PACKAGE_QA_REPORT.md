## Internal consistency review

The package consistently expresses one business model:

> **Near-zero-cost diagnosis → fixed-price Proof Activation → high-value Proof Mission → recurring Proof Office → protected private capability invocation → network economics.**

The following operating boundaries are maintained across the artifacts:

- model output is candidate work, not authority;
- payment establishes commercial relevance, not proof of value;
- Evidence Dockets record the full denominator;
- independent review challenges material claims;
- Chronicle governs what may become institutional memory;
- Merkle roots commit state but do not create truth by themselves;
- customer value is separated into forecast, verified, and realized states;
- private capability is monetized through governed invocation rather than transferred by default;
- financial figures are management hypotheses, not realized outcomes or guarantees.

## Claim and professional boundary

This package is a strategic and operating-plan artifact. It is not:

- an audited forecast;
- a valuation opinion;
- a securities offering document;
- legal, tax, accounting, investment, or regulatory advice;
- a security audit or certification;
- a guarantee of customer ROI, product-market fit, financing, or enterprise value.

Any customer-facing economic claim should be connected to an inspectable attribution method and should not be represented as realized until the underlying value has actually occurred.
