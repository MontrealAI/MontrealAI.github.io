# GoalOS Optimal Business Model — Sources and Methodology

## Purpose
This package converts the GoalOS institutional thesis into an explicit commercial model: acquisition, pricing, delivery, recurring revenue, private capability, five-year management scenarios, proof gates and a 90-day launch.

## Evidence standard
External sources support the timing, infrastructure availability, market context, selected comparative benchmarks and regulatory environment. GoalOS product prices, volumes, margins, customer ROI, financing, valuation implications and operating targets are management hypotheses. They are not audited forecasts, realized results, legal advice, investment advice, tax advice, security certification or guarantees.

## Financial methodology
The workbook separates volume, price, direct cost, operating expense, financing, working capital, capital expenditure and cash taxes. The base case shifts revenue from one-time missions toward Proof Offices, private networks, capability invocation and network fees. Every assumption is editable.

## Customer value methodology
Value is classified as forecast, independently supported, and realized. GoalOS should not present forecast value as realized ROI. Standard offers normally require a conservative value pool of at least four times the first-year fee.

## Institutional boundary
Model output is candidate work. Evidence establishes what occurred. Independent validation challenges material claims. Chronicle governs whether accepted capability may influence future missions. Merkle roots commit state but do not create truth by themselves. Private intelligence remains protected or encrypted; the customer receives governed outcomes and receipts, not the crown-jewel implementation.

## Public claim doctrine
Maximum ambition. Minimum unsupported claim. Payment earns relevance. Outcomes earn credibility. Independent verification earns authority. Repeat purchase earns product confidence. Fresh transfer earns the platform.
