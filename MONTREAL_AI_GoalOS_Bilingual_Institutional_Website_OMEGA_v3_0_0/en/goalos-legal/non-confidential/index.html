NON-CONFIDENTIAL COMMUNICATIONS POLICY

MONTREAL.AI’s public surface is for fit qualification, not for receiving secrets.

◆PUBLIC FIRSTPROTECTED AFTER AGREEMENT
Important. Do not send confidential, personal, privileged, regulated, classified, security-sensitive, proprietary, or third-party information before an applicable written agreement is signed and an approved channel is provided.

1. Unsolicited communications

Unsolicited emails, messages, local forms, posts, connection requests, attachments, or other communications create no duty of confidentiality, engagement, attorney-client relationship, advisory relationship, review obligation, conflict, exclusivity, or service commitment.

2. Permitted pre-engagement content

  • General decision category
  • Sponsor role and organization type
  • Approximate value-at-stake or cost-of-error range
  • General deadline and desired proof level
  • Information already public and lawfully shareable

3. Prohibited pre-engagement content

  • Trade secrets, source code, proprietary or non-public data
  • Personal, customer, employee, patient, or user information
  • Financial, health, biometric, security, or other regulated data
  • Passwords, tokens, keys, seed phrases, credentials, or vulnerabilities
  • Privileged documents or materials subject to litigation, investigation, or preservation duties
  • Third-party information without a right to disclose

4. Activating a protected channel

Once fit is established, MONTREAL.AI may require an NDA, MSA, SOW, DPA, security schedule, intellectual-property schedule, publication permission, retention policy, and approved technical environment before receiving detailed materials.

5. No assurance of acceptance

MONTREAL.AI may decline, delete, not review, return, or request reformulation of any communication. Sending an idea, proposal, or objective grants no right, priority, compensation, or response obligation.

6. Version and review

Public version v3.0.0, July 20, 2026. This policy is an operating template subject to qualified counsel review before commercial activation.
