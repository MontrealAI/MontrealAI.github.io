# Formal Model and Governance

## Launch Mission

`M = (O, S, B, τ, R, A, F, Π, E)`

- `O`: consequential objective
- `S`: accountable sponsor and stakeholders
- `B`: approved budget and available capital
- `τ`: time horizon and deadlines
- `R`: risk, control and reversibility posture
- `A`: authority and permission model
- `F`: functions to optimize
- `Π`: policy and regulatory constraints
- `E`: evidence available at mission start

## Institutional Scenario

`s = (r, J, L, K, P, T, G, Q)`

The scenario contains the institutional root, function-to-jurisdiction mapping, legal/ownership graph, capital sequence, public-support portfolio, controlled transactions, AGI Job graph and continuing-governance plan.

## Verified Launch Value

GoalOS maximizes the complete risk-adjusted denominator—market, capital, public support, talent, infrastructure, tax and capability value less formation, operating, coordination, compliance, dilution, delay, risk and unwind costs—only among scenarios that pass evidence and authority gates.

## Constitutional laws

- **Proof before authority. Evidence before memory.**
- **Transaction Law:** if an applicable receipt is missing, stale, unattributable or unauthorized, the material transaction remains blocked.
- **Runway Law:** no unsigned investment, unawarded grant, unassessed credit, unapproved loan or speculative customer contract enters base runway.
- **Succession Law:** reuse is not improvement; a successor earns authority only through a fresh comparison with no governance regression.
- **Final Institutional Law:** no objective, no mission; no facts, no result; no real dependency, no graph edge; no Evidence Docket, no value claim; no responsible authority, no transaction; no Chronicle admission, no future influence; no fresh transfer, no improvement claim; no verified contribution, no productive reinvestment.
