# 1. Why this can become a new category

The existing market is fragmented into highly focused products:

* Stripe Atlas forms Delaware companies, obtains EINs, issues founder equity and files 83(b) elections; Stripe says more than 100,000 founders from over 140 countries have used it, at a current one-time price of US$500. ([Stripe][1])
* Remote enables employer-of-record hiring in more than 90 countries, currently listing a standard US-dollar price of US$699 per employee per month. ([Remote][2])
* Deel says it owns entities and infrastructure in more than 100 countries for global employment operations. ([Deel][3])
* Carta supports cap tables, equity issuance, SAFE financings, valuations, investor onboarding, KYC/AML, contracts and fund administration. ([Carta][4])
* Doola exposes company formation through an API, including formation, EIN and registered-agent services, with partner pricing advertised from US$149 per entity. ([doola: A Business-in-a-Box™][5])

These products demonstrate demand for individual launch functions.

But the founder, board, public institution or project sponsor must still answer the harder questions:

```text
Which country should be the institutional root?
Where should we sell?
Where should we hire?
Where should we perform R&D?
Where should capability and IP reside?
Which entity should raise which capital?
Which grants and fiscal incentives can lawfully stack?
Where should infrastructure be deployed?
What sequence minimizes dilution, tax, delay and risk?
Which transactions require local professional authority?
When should we restructure, expand, repair or stop?
```

No single existing application owns that objective end to end.
