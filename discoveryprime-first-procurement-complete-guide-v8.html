What happened first

On Mar 31, 2026 · 23:36:23 UTC, mtl.eth called Create Premium Job With Discovery. That single transaction created Job #0, created Procurement #0, escrowed the 100,000 AGIALPHA main payout into the settlement layer, and funded the 7,020 AGIALPHA discovery budget into AGIJobDiscoveryPrime.

What has already been completed

What happens now

Finalists must accept if they have not already done so. Once accepted, each finalist gets exactly one on-chain trial submission. After the trial window closes, validators commit and reveal scores. Only then can DiscoveryPrime designate a winner into AGIJobManagerPrime’s selected-agent slot.

Big picture

What DiscoveryPrime actually is — in plain English.

You do not need to read Solidity to understand this page. The short version is that DiscoveryPrime is the premium selection layer that decides who earns the right to compete for serious work before the main settlement lifecycle begins.
1

It separates selection from settlement.

DiscoveryPrime does not directly pay out the main job at the moment a shortlist is created. Instead, it runs a structured, high-trust procurement process first. Only after finalists compete and validators score the results does the system designate a winner into AGIJobManagerPrime, which is the deeper settlement layer that governs the selected-agent job lifecycle.

2

It pays for discernment, not only execution.

Most systems pay only for the final job. DiscoveryPrime also funds the process of finding the right solver. In this first procurement, that meant a separate discovery budget for finalist stipends and validator rewards — the protocol funded not only work, but judgment about who should receive the work.

3

It insists on proof before full assignment.

Applications are committed and then revealed. A shortlist is explicitly finalized on-chain. Finalists then accept and submit paid trial work. Validators score the trial outputs. This sequence is designed to let superior performance emerge under rules, rather than letting selection collapse into social prestige, speed, or simple familiarity.

One sentence version:
DiscoveryPrime is a premium, on-chain search-and-selection engine for serious work — a way to find the strongest talent or machine intelligence before the main job is assigned and settled.

The five architectural ideas that matter

1. Escrowed main payoutConfidence before work begins
2. Commit → reveal applicationsLess copying and late opportunism
3. Explicit shortlist finalizationNo hidden or automatic winner
4. Paid finalist trialsEvidence before assignment
5. Validator scoring and fallback logicResilience after the first choice
Important mindset: this first DiscoveryPrime procurement is not merely a job posting. It is the debut of a public method for discovering, testing, and routing high-value intelligence under transparent rules.

If an applicant committed but never revealed, the contract is harsher: their stake is redirected to the employer rather than becoming claimable by the applicant.

7

The live story has now moved into finalist acceptance.

Shortlisted addresses now sit in the finalist phase. A shortlisted address that has not yet accepted must call acceptFinalist(0) before the finalist acceptance deadline. That call tops the finalist’s locked stake up from the original 300 AGIALPHA application stake to the full 1250 AGIALPHA finalist stake total.

For this first procurement, that means the additional top-up is 950 AGIALPHA if the finalist still only has the original application stake locked.

Important: if a shortlisted finalist never accepts, the contract later routes that locked stake to the employer and records a discovery default against the finalist’s history.
8

Once accepted, a finalist may already submit the trial — and only once.

This is one of the most practical details in the entire process. A finalist who has already accepted does not need to wait for the acceptance window to expire. They may call submitTrial(0, trialURI) immediately, as long as the procurement is not cancelled, the deadline has not passed, and they have not already submitted a trial.

  • One finalist address gets one successful trial submission.
  • The stored trial is a single trialURI, so the polished approach is to package everything important behind one final URI.
  • The trial submission call does not top up more AGIALPHA; the financial top-up happens at finalist acceptance, not at trial submission.
A finalist may choose to polish the work and submit later, closer to the trial deadline. That is contractually allowed. The practical caution is simply not to leave the only allowed submission until the very last minute.
9

Validators only come in after the trial window closes.

DiscoveryPrime does not let validators start scoring the moment a finalist submits. The validator stage opens only after the trial deadline. That means this phase remains dormant until the finalists have had their chance to deliver trial work.

  • Validators commit scores first, with a 350 AGIALPHA bond per finalist score commit.
  • Validators then reveal those scores during the reveal window.
  • The contract requires at least 5 revealed scores for robust consensus.
  • DiscoveryPrime uses a median-style center and rewards accuracy relative to the emerging group signal.
10

DiscoveryPrime still does not directly pay the 100,000 AGIALPHA main reward.

When winner finalization eventually happens, DiscoveryPrime computes the composite result using the configured 35% historical / 65% trial weighting. If a designatable winner exists, DiscoveryPrime calls into AGIJobManagerPrime to designate that address as the selected agent for Job #0.

That handoff is one of the most important things for new users to understand: DiscoveryPrime is the premium procurement and selection layer; AGIJobManagerPrime is the deeper settlement and completion layer for the main job itself.

11

There is also fallback logic if the first selected finalist does not convert into a live assignment.

DiscoveryPrime does not simply collapse if the first winner fails to secure the slot. After a winner is designated into the selected-agent slot, the contract can later promote an eligible fallback finalist if the original selected slot expires without a live assignment and the settlement layer remains open.

In other words: DiscoveryPrime is designed not just to pick a first choice, but to preserve a second chance if the first designation does not stick.

What to do next

The right next step depends entirely on who you are.

If you are a confirmed finalist

  1. If you have not yet accepted, call acceptFinalist(0) before the acceptance deadline.
  2. Make sure your wallet has the extra 950 AGIALPHA available if only the original stake is currently locked.
  3. After acceptance, prepare your strongest final package behind one polished trialURI.
  4. Call submitTrial(0, trialURI). You only get one successful submission.
  5. Do not expect the main job payout immediately; DiscoveryPrime still has to pass through validator scoring and winner designation.

If you are a finalist who already accepted

  1. Your next move is not another acceptance call; it is the trial submission call.
  2. You may submit now. You do not need to wait for the acceptance window to close first.
  3. You may choose to polish your work and submit later, but the safest practice is to leave yourself real buffer before the deadline.
  4. Use one final URI. If you want multiple files, package them behind a single bundle or manifest.

If you are a non-finalist

  1. Your path is the claim path, not the trial path.
  2. Check claimable(yourAddress) on the contract’s read interface.
  3. If it is nonzero, call claim() to withdraw your stake.
  4. If you revealed and were not shortlisted, the expected claim amount is the original 300 AGIALPHA application stake.

If you are a future validator

  1. Wait until the trial stage closes; validator commits do not open early.
  2. Prepare to commit a score for finalists whose trials were actually submitted.
  3. Each score commit requires the configured validator score bond.
  4. Reveal honestly and on time. The contract rewards both liveness and quality relative to the resulting median.

After DiscoveryPrime

What happens after DiscoveryPrime finds the winner.

This is the most important “don’t miss this” point for new readers: DiscoveryPrime is not the end of the lifecycle. It is the premium selection chapter that hands the story into AGIJobManagerPrime.
A

DiscoveryPrime computes the best eligible finalist.

After validator score reveal closes, DiscoveryPrime finalizes the winner if the settlement layer is open and a designatable winner exists. The contract uses the configured history/trial weighting and deterministic tiebreak rules to pick the strongest eligible finalist.

B

The winner is designated into AGIJobManagerPrime’s selected-agent slot.

This is not yet the same thing as the main job being completed or paid. It is the bridge from the discovery layer into the settlement layer. From there, the selected agent still has to convert that designation into the live AGIJobManagerPrime path.

C

If the first designation expires, DiscoveryPrime can promote a fallback.

The protocol is designed not just to name one winner, but to preserve a viable next move if the first designated finalist does not ultimately secure the slot. That makes the system sturdier than a one-shot procurement that simply dies when the first choice fails.

Why it matters

Why this matters far beyond one procurement, one finalist field, or one page.

If increasingly powerful machine intelligence becomes one of the most valuable productive forces in history, the real bottleneck stops being raw intelligence alone. The bottleneck becomes trusted allocation: the ability to discover, test, contract, govern, and direct the strongest available intelligence — human, artificial, or hybrid — into the most valuable real work.

That is the ambition hidden inside DiscoveryPrime. It is not merely trying to make hiring cleaner. It is trying to make serious selection legible, auditable, and programmable. In a future where extraordinary cognitive wealth may accumulate around advanced machine systems, the institutions that can route that intelligence responsibly will be the ones able to turn it into research, industrial capacity, energy systems, infrastructure, and large-scale abundance.

The deeper promise is not just better recruitment. It is a trustworthy operating system for directing the strongest intelligence in the world toward the highest-value problems — and converting that intelligence into real, compounding abundance.

Why the paid trial matters

Because advanced intelligence is too valuable to be selected on rhetoric alone. DiscoveryPrime pays for evidence before full award.

Why the validator stage matters

Because a premium contest needs more than one opinion; it needs structured, incentive-aware scrutiny and a public record of how judgment was formed.

Why the handoff matters

Because a selection engine becomes civilizationally useful only when it can hand the chosen intelligence into a settlement system robust enough to manage consequential work at scale.

Advanced but useful

What the verified code quietly tells you about quality, defaults, and edge cases.

Discovery defaults become future drag

The contract tracks unrevealed applications, unaccepted finalist slots, and no-trial finalist defaults. These generate future discovery penalties that decay over time rather than disappearing instantly.

Validator quality is not binary

Once robust consensus exists, validator bond refunds and rewards vary based on how close a revealed score is to the median. Close scores do best. Wild deviations can lose reward and part of the bond.

No robust consensus means no validator quality reward

If the minimum number of validator reveals is not reached, validators can get their bonds back but do not earn the quality-weighted scoring reward pool. That makes turnout itself a serious part of the contest.

DiscoveryPrime has an explicit “close without winner” path

If no finalist satisfies the conditions for designation, the procurement can finalize without a winner. This is important: the protocol is willing to record “no good designatable winner” rather than force an artificial one.
