The public operating doctrine

GoalOS generalizes autonomous improvement from one agent harness to a proof-gated intelligence institution. Every proposed improvement remains candidate work until the next evidentiary and governance gate passes.

01

Output is not authority

Models and agents may propose and act. Evidence, review, scope, freshness, rights, and rollback determine what may matter next.

02

Memory is gated

Only Chronicle-admitted, evidence-backed, scoped, replayable, and revocable capability may influence future missions.

03

Search is not truth

Opportunity scores and novelty allocate attention; they cannot bypass baselines, proof, risk, rights, or safety gates.

04

No Mission 2, no compounding

A fresh adjacent mission under comparable constraints must show that retained capability actually improves future work.

05

No rollback, no release

Every promoted capability retains an incumbent, canary boundary, stop condition, recovery route, and reauthorization gate.

06

Measured effect before capacity

Scenario value, modeled benefit, or token activity does not establish productive capacity. Real effects require real evidence.

Public publication architecture

The release publishes the doctrine and proof standards, places investment-sensitive material under separate review, and withholds confidential or security-sensitive working information.

Publish publicly

  • Proof, governance, safety, validation, and memory architecture
  • Claim-bounded research and public-safe demonstrations
  • Evidence status, correction, publication, and assurance mechanisms
  • Public Evidence Dockets as independent evidence matures

Separate review

  • $AGIALPHA economics, MARK, capital, treasury, and value-capture materials
  • Partnership, licensing, acquisition, and commercialization structures
  • Deployment-specific legal, tax, operator, and financial representations
  • Claims tied to live settlement, adoption, price, liquidity, or realized returns

Withhold

  • Secrets, keys, signers, recovery, incidents, and exploitable security detail
  • Privileged advice, customer data, private reviewer notes, and confidential inventions
  • Editable internal working sources and operator-specific records
  • Any material lacking confirmed rights, approval, or accurate claim boundaries
