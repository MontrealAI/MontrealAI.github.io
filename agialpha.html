Proof‑First Ascension

No value without evidence.
No autonomy without authority.
No settlement without validation.

Agents may execute. Markets may route. Jobs may complete. But value moves only when evidence survives validation. The archive preserves what worked so future mandates start stronger. This is the difference between raw automation and governed compounding capability.

Job SpecArtifactTestHashAttestationValidationSettlementArchive

  1. Job Spec: Goal, scope, inputs, acceptance criteria, authority.
  2. Artifact: Code, report, data, workflow, model output, or operational result.
  3. Test: Automated or human-reviewed criteria proving the artifact satisfies the mandate.
  4. Hash: Tamper-evident record of the work product and proof bundle.
  5. Attestation: Signed statement by agent, validator, node, or council.
  6. Validation: Evidence review, replay, challenge, or approval path.
  7. Settlement: Value movement only after proof passes.
  8. Archive: Reusable recipe becomes institutional memory.

AGI.Eth Namespace Grammar

One rule. Scoped meaning. Machine-readable authority.

Main grammar

<entity>.( <env>. )<role>.agi.eth

role ∈ {club, agent, node} · env ∈ ENV_SET, optional; examples: alpha, x, …

Examples

  • alice.club.agi.eth
  • alice.alpha.club.agi.eth
  • helper.agent.agi.eth
  • helper.alpha.agent.agi.eth
  • gpu01.node.agi.eth
  • gpu01.alpha.node.agi.eth

Sovereigns / AGI Businesses

  • <sovereign>.agi.eth
  • env.agi.eth → alpha.agi.eth, x.agi.eth
  • <business>.env.agi.eth → ops.alpha.agi.eth

Scope rule: entity.env.role.agi.eth is recognized and developed only within env.agi.eth.

Environment package and aliases

  • env.agi.eth
  • env.agent.agi.eth
  • env.node.agi.eth
  • env.club.agi.eth
  • agent.env.agi.eth
  • node.env.agi.eth
  • club.env.agi.eth

Optional aliases are env-local and not official by default unless explicitly whitelisted.
