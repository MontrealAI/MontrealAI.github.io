Static perimeter

This public edition calls no RPC, requests no signature, reads no wallet, deploys no contract, transfers no asset and creates no on-chain entitlement.

General risks

Smart contracts, wallets, keys, chains, oracles, bridges, tokens, ENS, Name Wrapper, distributed storage and external services may be buggy, compromised, congested, censored, reorganized, unavailable, misconfigured or irreversible.

Merkle root

A root proves relative integrity of a canonical state; it does not alone prove truth, safety, legality, quality, human identity, commercial rights or future validity.

Confidentiality

A public chain does not keep plaintext private. Sensitive data must remain encrypted, off-chain or inside a protected execution boundary. Encryption depends on keys, cryptographic assumptions, rotation, access and incident handling.

Direct name

Current direct-name ownership may be necessary for an action, but is not sufficient for admission, use entitlement or compounding. Transfers, expiries, fuses, wrappers, compromised keys and stale sessions must be handled.

Separate activation

Any production Web3 functionality requires contract audit, cryptographic review, key governance, controlled Mainnet testing, operational security, incident procedures, regulatory analysis and suitable confirmation UX.
