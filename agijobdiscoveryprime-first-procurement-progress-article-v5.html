Why this opening round is different

History matters in the design, but it does not yet separate this field.

DiscoveryPrime is built to reward serious prior performance over time. But in the specific situation described for this first-ever AGI job, the five applicants are being treated here as having no historical DiscoveryPrime advantage. That changes the meaning of the next step.

At shortlist time, the contract does not rank revealed application prose. It ranks by historical score first. If the field is indeed flat at zero history, the shortlist becomes a deterministic tie-break rather than a legacy-driven ranking. Then, once finalists are chosen, the process becomes qualitative again through paid trials and validator scoring.

In plain English: this first round is the bootstrap round. It is not yet a contest of long-term DiscoveryPrime history. It is the round that begins creating that history.

Zero history does not mean zero standards

Applicants still had to clear protocol eligibility, authorization, and settlement-side checks to enter. “No historical advantage” is not the same thing as “no gatekeeping.”

What the shortlist is really doing now

In this particular opening field, the shortlist is choosing who earns the right to perform a paid trial, not declaring who has already won on past record.

What will actually decide the winner

The winner is still selected with a 35% history / 65% trial blend. But if finalists remain equal on history, the trial result effectively becomes the decisive signal.

What happens now

The next chapter is procedural first, then competitive.

DiscoveryPrime now moves through a very specific sequence. First the reveal window closes. Then the shortlist is finalized. Then the real competitive separation begins: finalists accept, submit paid trials, validators score, and only after that does a winner emerge.

1 · End of reveal

Wait for the reveal window to close

Nothing else should be decided before the reveal deadline passes. This protects the integrity of the stage and keeps late valid reveals from being excluded.

5 · Validator scoring and handoff

Validators decide whether a winner can exist

Validators commit scores, then reveal them. Each score uses a 350 AGIALPHA bond, and each reveal can earn up to 120 AGIALPHA. A finalist needs at least 5 revealed validator scores to remain eligible. If no finalist reaches that threshold, the procurement can close without a designated winner.

Commit closes Apr 14 · reveal closes Apr 15 · winner finalization follows
Validator turnout is not decorative; it is decisive

What the likely winner formula means in this first round

The contract still uses a 35% history / 65% trial blend. But if all finalists remain equal on historical score, the ordering of the finalists will be driven by trial score first, with lower address only breaking exact ties.

What happens after a winner is designated

The winning finalist is handed into the underlying AGIJobManagerPrime job with a 3-day selected acceptance window and a 2-day checkpoint window. The main job itself was created with a 7-day duration.

Why this round matters beyond this one job

This is the round that starts creating DiscoveryPrime history.

Before this procurement, the system could describe how history would matter. After this procurement, the system begins to actually accumulate it. That is a meaningful transition.

  • Applicants who reveal, accept, and submit on time begin to establish a visible pattern of reliability.
  • Applicants who fail to reveal, fail to accept, or fail to submit trials begin to accumulate discovery defaults that can matter later.
  • Validators begin establishing whether they can produce enough honest, timely turnout for the model to function smoothly.
  • Future rounds will no longer be clean-slate rounds in the same way, because this one begins the public memory of who followed through.

Questions that will matter to careful readers

A concise FAQ for this stage of the story.

Did the creation transaction actually succeed?

Yes. The creation transaction shows Status: Success, and the page displays the expected JobCreated, ProcurementCreated, and PremiumJobCreated event logs. The extra warning line on Etherscan does not change the fact that the procurement was created.

Why is the projected shortlist based on address order?

Because under the assumptions given for this update, the five applicants are all flat on historical score. In the contract, tied historical scores are broken deterministically by lower wallet address. That is why this opening shortlist can be projected mechanically.

Does zero history mean zero standards?

No. It means zero historical advantage inside the DiscoveryPrime ranking logic for this first round. Applicants still had to satisfy eligibility and authorization checks handled by the live contracts.

What could still prevent a winner from being designated?

Several things. A shortlisted finalist might fail to accept. A finalist might accept but fail to submit the trial. Or validators might fail to produce at least five revealed scores for any finalist. In those cases, the procurement can move forward without a winner.
